<h5>Design / Manufacturing / Programming</h5>
<ul>
<li>We use Solidworks computer-aided design (CAD) software to model our robots.</li>
<li>​Our members machine and assemble our robots in-house, often using our CNC mill. </li>
<li>We program robot controls, autonomous driving routines, and other applications in Java.</li>
</ul>
<h5>Business / Outreach / Media</h5>
<ul>
<li>We raise and manage tens of thousands of dollars, much like a real business.</li>
<li>We build team awareness with marketing and media, interacting with companies and our communities.</li>
<li>We host fundraising events and robotics camps for youth.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Your First Season</h2>
<p>									<a href="">Offseason Competitions</a><br />
									<a href="">Internal Robotics Competition</a><br />
									<a href="">Build Season</a><br />
									Offseason Competitions</p>
<p><img loading="lazy" src="/wp-content/uploads/2020/07/P1010374-300x225.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="225" />During the Fall semester, you&#8217;ll have the opportunity to attend regional offseason competitions, where you&#8217;ll be able to get a feel for what it&#8217;s like to be at a FIRST robotics competition. You&#8217;ll also be able to meet people from other FRC teams!</p>
<p>									Internal Robotics Competition</p>
<p><img loading="lazy" src="/wp-content/uploads/2020/07/IMG_9483-300x200.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="200" />The Internal Robotics Competition (IRC) is a rookie competition that we host at Lowell for all new members of the team. The purpose of the IRC is to help rookies familiarize themselves in our work environment, teach them how to use mechanical tools in the shop, and allow them to gain firsthand experience in competing for a robotics competition. Rookies will be placed in teams of 4-5, and they&#8217;ll have about 8 weeks to fully design, prototype and program a functioning robot to compete in the IRC. Each team will be given the same materials to work with, which we call the kit of parts (KOP). Rookies will also receive guidance from mentors through workshops in all subteam topics, which include CAD, programming, and more! </p>
<p>									Build Season</p>
<p><img loading="lazy" src="/wp-content/uploads/2020/07/IMG_2108-300x200.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="200" />During Build Season, rookies will be able to shadow veterans in the subteam(s) of their choice and help out with building Field Elements, programming, and more! They will gain firsthand experience in their preferred subteams by working under veterans to further the progress of our robot.</p>
