<h3>by Derek Chan</h3>
<p>&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;The FRC 2015 game, Recycle Rush, features two alliances stacking totes and containers and utilizing pool noodles&nbsp;in order to gain as many points possible. Many robotics fans <a href="https://www.reddit.com/r/FRC/comments/2r7m99/this_game_is_garbage/">complained</a> <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hTyCIYZQ_1s">that</a> <a href="http://www.chiefdelphi.com/forums/showthread.php?t=131911&amp;page=4">the game</a> was not competitive, not exciting as a spectator sport,&nbsp;and, besides the Coopertition points during the qualifying matches,&nbsp;involved very&nbsp;little interaction between the opposing alliances. However, careful readers of the game manual have discovered a breakthrough that could bring a new element of cooperation, trust, and even betrayal in this&nbsp;seemingly divided game.</p>
<p>      <img src="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/53692533e4b04e767ae9a4f6/53695d37e4b072d605cf584d/54b456e4e4b0e5630554a852/1421104999363/noodle_incident.gif" alt=""/></p>
<p>&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;Due to a loophole in the rules, human players of each alliance could purposely throw litter on their own side of the field for an easy 4&nbsp;points per litter for the other team. If alliances both coordinate before hand, the alliances could earn points for each other, providing that the alliances agreed beforehand,&nbsp;giving these alliances&nbsp;an edge over those who do not honor the Noodle Agreement. Compared with the difficult task of putting a noodle on the containers, the Noodle Agreement is possibly the most efficient method to earn points with the litter.&nbsp;However, this sets up an interesting Prisoner&#8217;s Dilemma, where an alliance could possibly backstab the other team by pushing the noodles in the landfill at the last second&nbsp;for even&nbsp;more points for themselves (1 per litter in landfill). The Noodle Agreement would bring an interesting&nbsp;new aspect&nbsp;of collaboration&nbsp;in FRC.</p>
<p>&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;Forum members on Chief Delphi and Reddit exploded on the idea, even coming to the point where <a href="http://www.chiefdelphi.com/forums/showthread.php?t=132516">contract slips</a> were being made to ensure mutual agreement. However, FIRST apparently did not like the concept of mutual collusion in FRC. On January 9th, 2015, FIRST <a href="http://www.usfirst.org/sites/default/files/uploadedFiles/Robotics_Programs/FRC/Game_and_Season__Info/2015/2015TeamUpdates0109.pdf">updated the rules</a>, which would eliminate the Noodle Agreement. Pool noodles will&nbsp;now be color coded and will only award points if the litter is of the opposing team&#8217;s color. Now, human players have to throw their litter to the other alliance and remain there in order to gain the 4 unprocessed litter points.&nbsp;Granted that teams could still agree to not push opposing&nbsp;litter to the landfill, this &#8220;<a href="http://www.chiefdelphi.com/forums/showthread.php?threadid=132512">Newdle Agreement</a>&#8221; is nowhere near as interesting nor as easy to pull off.</p>
<p>&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;The Noodle Agreement was an interesting case of loopholing the rules and brought an interesting twist to FRC. As FIRST claims that FRC is more than&nbsp;robots, the Noodle Agreement&nbsp;brought economic game theory, such as&nbsp;collusion and incentive to cheat,&nbsp;and lawyer skills, such as&nbsp;carefully reading rules to find exploits, in the world of FRC. Although short-lived, the Noodle Agreement had the potential to bring an interesting new element in a seemingly simple and drab game.</p>
<p>In honor of the Noodle Agreement</p>
