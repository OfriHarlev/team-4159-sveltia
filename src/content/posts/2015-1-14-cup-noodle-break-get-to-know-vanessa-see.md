<![endif]--><!--StartFragment--><strong>How long have you been a member of the team?</strong></p>
<p>Two years.</p>
<p><strong>What is your favorite pastime?</strong></p>
<p>I love to read! My favorite author is Haruki Murakami. He writes metaphysical novels (so I don’t really know what’s going on, but that’s what I love about his books).</p>
<p><strong>What is your favorite band?</strong></p>
<p>Arcade Fire—they’re this insanely talented, passionate band from Quebec.</p>
<p><strong>What got you interested in robotics?</strong></p>
<p>In the 10th grade, I sat next to a robotics team member, Matthew Chai, in geometry class and he told me about robotics and it sounded really interesting. Also, I saw articles in the school newspaper about the team, and in the pictures, there were very few girls, so I wanted to join to change that.</p>
<p><!--EndFragment--></p>
<p><strong>What is your favorite thing about robotics?</strong></p>
<p>The friends I’ve made! I’ve made some of my best friends through robotics, since there’s nothing that makes a friendship stronger than staying at school until 9 at night during build season! Also, I’ve always liked math and science, but engineering was just some thing that I knew existed but had never considered, so I’m grateful that robotics has helped me discover my passion for engineering.</p>
<p><strong>Any final comments?</strong></p>
<p>If I hadn’t joined robotics, I probably wouldn’t have discovered my passion for engineering, had an internship with a tech company, or applied to Cornell to study engineering, but all these things happened to me (and I’m Cornell c/o 2019!), so I’m forever grateful that I did join.</p>
<p>I encourage people to stick with robotics or to join, because it’s not just about robots—it’s about friendship, volunteering, learning, mentoring, and growing—and through robotics, anything can happen!</p>
